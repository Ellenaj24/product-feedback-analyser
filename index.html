Product Manager · Case Study

Turning customer noise into prioritised product opportunities.

A repeatable workflow I designed to consolidate customer feedback from multiple channels, surface what matters most, and feed evidence-backed recommendations into the roadmap. Built with Qualtrics, Excel and Microsoft Copilot.

RoleProduct Manager
StackQualtrics · Excel · Copilot
OutputWorkflow + scoring framework
TypeInternal product / process
01 · The problem

Feedback was everywhere. Insight was nowhere.

Customer feedback was arriving through surveys, support tickets, sales conversations and reviews — each living in a different tool, owned by a different team. Volume made it impossible to read everything. Recency bias took over. The roadmap was being shaped by whoever spoke loudest in the last meeting, not by what customers were actually telling us.

The real problem wasn't a lack of feedback. It was the absence of a repeatable way to synthesise it, prioritise it, and turn it into decisions.
02 · The workflow

From raw comment to roadmap input, in ten steps.

I designed an end-to-end pipeline that any PM on the team could pick up and run. Each step has a clear owner, a clear output, and a clear handoff to the next.

01

Collect

Feedback gathered via Qualtrics surveys, plus exports from support, app stores and interview notes.

02

Consolidate

All feedback imported into a single Excel workbook — one row per piece of feedback.

03

Clean

Duplicates removed, dates standardised, off-topic responses filtered out.

04

Categorise

Each row tagged with product area, customer segment and feedback type. Copilot drafts; PM reviews.

05

Detect themes

Copilot clusters semantically similar comments. PM confirms, splits or merges the candidate themes.

06

Identify pain points

Themes converted into named pain points with severity and frequency counts.

07

Generate opportunities

Each pain point translated into a customer-outcome-led opportunity statement.

08

Prioritise

Opportunities scored against a six-criterion framework, weighted for strategic alignment.

09

Report

Stakeholder summary and PM working summary generated for the monthly cadence review.

10

Feed roadmap

Top opportunities enter backlog grooming and quarterly planning, with full evidence trail.

03 · The prioritisation framework

A scoring model that starts conversations, not ends them.

Every opportunity is scored 1–5 against six criteria. Strategic alignment is weighted higher because it's the one that most often gets quietly ignored. Effort is weighted lower because it's the easiest to mis-estimate early.

CriterionWhat it measuresWeight
FrequencyHow often this came up across feedback1.0
SeverityHow painful for the customer when it occurs1.0
Customer impactBreadth across segments and product areas1.0
Strategic alignmentFit with current product and company strategy1.5
Confidence in evidenceQuality and consistency of supporting feedback1.0
Implementation effortInverse — lower effort scores higher0.5
Score formula (Freq × 1.0) + (Sev × 1.0) + (Impact × 1.0) + (Strategic × 1.5) + (Confidence × 1.0) + (Effort × 0.5) Maximum possible: 30. Treat the score as a starting point, not a verdict.
04 · The outcome

What changed.

The team moved from anecdotal prioritisation to a defensible, evidence-backed process. Every opportunity in the backlog could now be traced back to the original customer voices that produced it. Stakeholder conversations shifted from "what do you think we should build?" to "here is what 47 customers told us, grouped into 6 themes, ranked by impact."

Single source of truth

All feedback consolidated into one workbook with a consistent taxonomy.

Repeatable rhythm

Weekly hygiene, monthly synthesis, quarterly roadmap input — runnable by any PM.

Evidence trail

Every opportunity links back to the verbatim comments that justify it.

Faster decisions

Less time arguing about what customers want. More time deciding what to do about it.

05 · Reflection

What I'd do differently.

Copilot is a fast first-draft analyst, not a final one. Early on I trusted its clustering more than I should have, and a few themes ended up too broad to be actionable. The fix was to add a PM review gate after every Copilot step and to keep the tag glossary tight. If I were starting again, I'd build that review gate in from day one rather than retrofitting it.

I'd also instrument the workflow itself sooner — tracking how long each step takes, which themes recur month after month, and which opportunities get acted on versus shelved. That longitudinal view is where the real organisational learning lives.
